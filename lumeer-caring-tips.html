Gold Collection
How To Care For
Your Gold Pieces
Tap each tip to learn more.
1
Remove Prior To Swimming Or Entering The Sea
Ensure the item is not exposed to saltwater or prolonged moisture. Only tap water contact is allowed.
Note: Remove your pieces before swimming (chlorine) and showering (shampoo chemicals) to prevent peeling and discoloration.
2
Avoid Contact With Chemicals And Alcohol-Based Substances
Refrain from exposure to perfumes, lotions, and harsh detergents to preserve integrity.
Note: Chemicals or alcohol may cause discoloration (turn black) or fading over time.
3
Reduce Abrasive Contact
Take off your pieces when exercising to preserve its pristine finish and prevent scratches.
Note: Make it the final step of your getting-ready routine, and the first thing you take off at the end of the day.
4
Clean Delicately
Rinse with water, then gently wipe with a soft microfiber or jewelry cloth to remove oils, sweat, and residue after each wear.
Note: Do not soak. Proper cleaning prevents oxidation and maintains material quality.
5
Store With Care
Store pieces individually in a dry place or in the provided zip lock bag, away from direct sunlight.
Note: Proper storage prevents loss and keeps your pieces easy to access anytime.
Silver Collection
How To Care For
Your Silver Pieces
Tap each tip to learn more.
1
Keep It Dry
Silver can handle a splash, but try not to soak it — take it off before swimming or anything steamy to keep it looking its best.
2
Avoid Harsh Stuff
Perfume, lotion, sunscreen — they're great for you, not so great for your silver. Give it a moment before putting your jewellery on.
3
Reduce Friction
Let your jewellery breathe — avoid letting them bump or rub against keys, coins, or other pieces so they stay smooth and happy.
4
Polish Regularly
Use the silver polish cloth we've included to gently wipe it down and keep that fresh, glowing look — like it just got back from a mini spa.
5
Store With Care
Tuck your pieces into the ziplock or pouch provided when not wearing them — it keeps the shine longer and stops them from getting moody with the air.
